▸ THE HOOK

DeckShift pulls the deck-driven decision-making of Slay the Spire and Balatro, the kinetic platforming of Dead Cells, Hollow Knight, and Super Meat Boy, and binds them together with one core constraint:

Every jump costs Shift.

Movement becomes economy. Wasting jumps now means dying later. Cards aren't just for combat — they're tools for traversal. Create Platform, Dash, and others all draw from the same resource pool. The player is always managing two interlocking decks: their hand, and the level itself.

▸ One minute of DeckShift gameplay — cards, movement, hub.

★ MOVEMENT AS RESOURCE

"Shift" is a finite resource that drains with every jump and carries between rooms. The platforming becomes a puzzle of optimization, not just reflex.

★ HYBRID DECKBUILDING

Cards are dual-purpose: combat AND traversal. Fireball hurts enemies, Create Platform rewrites the level geometry, Floor is Lava inverts gravity. All draw from the same resource pool.

★ HUB SANDBOX

A consequence-free hub room where players can test cards and movement combos. Reachable via a single marker-component pattern that gates every resource cost in the game.

▸ TECHNICAL HIGHLIGHTS

  • Polymorphic card-action system. Refactored a 100-line dispatch switch into a CardActionExecutor with one class per action and explicit conflict-flag declarations, so overlapping card effects coexist without corrupting shared player state.
  • Hub sandbox via marker-component pattern. A single GameObject component + one IsCurrentRoomHub() method gates every resource-consumption site in the codebase, giving players a no-consequence space to learn cards.
  • Custom 2D camera pipeline. Replaced Cinemachine with a zone-based follow system (hysteresis transitions, world-space shake offsets) to resolve confiner issues with non-rectangular rooms.
  • Data-driven content systems. Cards, quests, and relics are all ScriptableObjects; new content requires zero engineering once the action/event hooks exist.
  • Quest + relic systems. Event-based progress tracking, live HUD overlays, multiple grant pathways (shop, gambling NPC, debug), all wired to the same data-driven backbone.
  • Engineering pragmatism. Project lives in a single canonical CLAUDE.md and per-session change log; every architectural rule comes with the story of the bug that taught it.
